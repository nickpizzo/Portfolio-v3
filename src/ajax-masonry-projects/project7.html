Concept and Tooling

Kitch.tv is a concept for a social video network for food and cooking enthusiasts. I designed and built the beginnings of the front end app, I helped to define the product by researching product market fit, and I design a pitch deck that was given to some interested parties (see it below). This project was built with React during a time when Webpack had significantly less adoption than it does today. Browserify with Gulp was used instead of webpack, and in my opinion this has become an inferior approach to tooling a React project. It is clear that Webpack attacks the build problem in a fundamentally more integrated and opinionated manner. In Browserify, Gulp/Grunt is used along with a long list of transforms and plugins to get the job done. Webpack offers enough power out of the box that you typically don’t need Grunt or Gulp at all. This is a case where configuration-based systems (Webpack) are preferable to imperative systems because Webpack makes relevant and useful assumptions about its projects up front.
